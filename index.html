Meet Miles

Miles is truly one-of-a-kind - a testament to nature's beautiful diversity. Born with a rare intersex condition, he has both male and female reproductive organs (testicles, penis, and internal ovaries). This makes Miles extraordinarily unique, as true hermaphroditism occurs in only 0.0012% of dogs - less than 1 in 83,000.

His amber coat and soulful eyes tell a story of survival and resilience that goes beyond his rare biological makeup. The combination of his intersex condition and his remarkable survival story makes him possibly one of the most unique dogs you'll ever encounter.

Found in a remote wilderness area, Miles likely survived on his own for nearly a year before coming to the shelter - a remarkable display of intelligence and determination. Due to his limited early socialization with humans, he's extremely shy and will need a patient, understanding adopter willing to work at his pace.

He's made slow but steady progress at our shelter, gradually learning to trust and eat in the presence of humans. At about 40 pounds, he's the perfect medium size - substantial but manageable in most living situations.

Pride Month: Celebrating Uniqueness

This Pride Month, we're celebrating Miles as a beautiful reminder that diversity exists naturally in all species. Just as Pride celebrates authenticity and the beauty of existing beyond traditional categories, Miles represents the wonderful spectrum that exists in nature.

Only a very small percentage of mammals are born with intersex conditions, making Miles truly exceptional from a biological perspective. We're looking for an adopter who doesn't just accept Miles' uniqueness, but celebrates it.

His journey to becoming a confident companion will take time, but for the right person willing to champion his cause, the reward of earning his trust will be immeasurable.
